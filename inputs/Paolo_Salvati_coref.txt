[1]Paolo Salvati (born 22 February 1939 in Rome – 24 June 2014 in Rome) was an Italian figurative artist, painter, and draftsman.
[2]Paolo Salvati landscapes are the expression of poetic art, characterized by an intense chromatic tone as a metaphorical depiction of the inner world of man.
[3]


== Biography ==
Paolo Salvati was born in Rome on 22 February 1939, in a house on Via Labicana.  
[4]Paolo Salvati  from 1967 exhibited a series of works in oil on canvas throughout cities in Sardinia such as Cagliari, Oristano, Bosa, Ghilarza, Paulilatino until 1969.
[5] 
[6]Since 1970 Paolo Salvati participated in exhibitions of plastic and figurative art in the church Trinità dei Monti and in Galleria Colonna renamed Galleria Alberto Sordi in Rome.
[7]
Paolo Salvati painted subjects figment of the imagination, such as Pietra Blu (English: Blue Stone) in 1973 and 1974, Sogni di Primavera d’Alta Montagna (English: Spring Dreams in High Mountain) in 1974, d’Estate (English: In Summer) in 1975, the paintings Alberi Blu (English: Blue Trees) in 1980, and Montagna Gialla (English: Yellow Mountain) in 1991; subsequently created a  series called Fronde Rosse (English: Red Fronds) in 1993, 1994, and 2000.
[8] Paolo Salvati also realized landscapes, seascapes, and portraits.
[9] 
[10]Paolo Salvati used the techniques colours oil on canvas, gouache, pastels, acrylic, and personally prepared canvases and frames which were used in Paolo Salvati's works.
[11]From 1977 to 1993 Paolo Salvati worked as a portrait painter in Piazza Navona in Rome; Paolo Salvati used pastels on paper, oil on canvas painting miniatures depicting Roman monuments and landscapes of fantasy, produced numerous portraits also oil on canvas.
Expert in designing and making frames on wooden base, research techniques to the preparation of adhesives or for the application of real gold and silver leaf.
[12] Paolo Salvati passion for guitar music concert lead Paolo Salvati to engage in lute making.
[13]

[14]Paolo Salvati became closer with painter and Paolo Salvati's maternal uncle  Gabriele Patriarca, and with Rinaldo Caressa (painter), Roberto Venturoni, with whom Paolo Salvati shared some of the exhibitions, such as Cento Pittori via Margutta and paint-off demonstration.
[15]In the summer of 1993 Paolo Salvati left Piazza Navona due to the encounters with a collector of works of art Don Agostino Chigi Albani della Rovere (1929–2002).In the 1990 Paolo Salvati won first prizes in different editions of paint-off: in December 1996 Paolo Salvati received the prize Artitalia at the Exhibition of Contemporary Art in Galleria l’Agostiniana in Rome; in 1995 Paolo Salvati exposed the painting Scogliera in Notturno (English: Reef at Night) oil on canvas 50×60; in August 2000 Paolo Salvati was awarded the first prize at the fourth edition of the competition of paint-off of Anticoli Corrado with a 50×60 oil on canvas painting depicting La Fonte Vecchia (English: The Old/Ancient Fount/Spring); between November 2000 and January 2001 Paolo Salvati performed at the Galleria Gesù e Maria in Via del Corso in Rome, at the beginning with a solo exhibition then participated in the exhibition of painting and sculpture “Jubilee Events in time and in the present” organized in order to celebrate the closing of the Great Jubilee.
[16]On 13 December 2005 is recognized illustrious citizen from the Lazio region.
[17]In 2006 Paolo Salvati met the critic Andrea De Liberi and Paolo Salvati's biography appeared in the journal Cultura published by Istituto Europeo delle Politiche Culturali ed Ambientali.
[18]In July 2009 in Trani Paolo Salvati knew the art critic Paolo Levi, then artistic director of the Catalogue of Modern Art Giorgio Mondadori, and participated in the Biennial of Contemporary Art, exhibition curated by Giuseppe De Nittis foundation which takes place in rooms used for the exhibitions in Castello Svevo.
[19]On 20 and 21 October 2012, the pilot Andrea Palma, during the trial and race in the national racetrack in Monza, displayed a copy of a portrait painted by Paolo Salvati  a color print positioned on the bonnet of the Ferrari 430.
[20]

[21]On 27 December 2012, on a proposal from the Presidency of the Council of Ministers, Paolo Salvati has been honoured with the title of Knight of the Order of Merit of the Italian Republic.
[22]In 2019 the Paolo Salvati Onlus association was legally established, which supports the memory of the artist and holds the Paolo Salvati Archive, for the updating of the general catalog of the works.
