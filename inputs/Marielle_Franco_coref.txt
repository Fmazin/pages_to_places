[1]Marielle Franco (Portuguese pronunciation: [maɾiˈɛli ˈfɾɐ̃ku]; born Marielle Francisco da Silva, 27 July 1979 – 14 March 2018) was a Brazilian politician, sociologist, feminist, socialist and human rights activist.
[2]Marielle Franco served as a city councillor of the Municipal Chamber of Rio de Janeiro for the Socialism and Liberty Party (PSOL) from January 2017 until Marielle Franco's assassination.

[3]On 14 March 2018, while in a car after delivering a speech in the north of Rio de Janeiro, Marielle Franco and Marielle Franco's driver were shot multiple times and killed by two murderers travelling in another vehicle.
[4]Marielle Franco had been an outspoken critic of police brutality and extrajudicial killings, as well as the February 2018 federal intervention by Brazilian president Michel Temer in the state of Rio de Janeiro which resulted in the deployment of the army in police operations.
[5]In March 2019, two former police officers were arrested and charged with the murder of Marielle Franco

[13]== Education ==
In 2000, Marielle Franco began Marielle Franco's pre-university studies.
[14]Following the death of a friend from a stray bullet in 2000, Marielle Franco began working in human rights activism.
[15]In 2002, Marielle Franco entered the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio de Janeiro on a scholarship and continued working and raising Marielle Franco's daughter as Marielle Franco worked towards a degree in social sciences.
[16]Marielle Franco subsequently earned a master's degree in public administration from the Fluminense Federal University.
[17]Marielle Franco master's thesis (titled "UPP: The Reduction of the Favela to Three Letters") concerns the law enforcement program to retake control of Rio's favelas from gangs.



[18]== Political career ==
Beginning in 2007, Marielle Franco worked as a consultant for the state representative Marcelo Freixo, and Marielle Franco coordinated the state legislature's Committee for the Defense of Human Rights and Citizenship.
[19]Marielle Franco also worked for civil society organizations, including the Brazil Foundation and the Maré Center for Solidarity Studies and Action.
[20]In 2016, Marielle Franco ran for a seat on the Rio de Janeiro city council in the municipal elections.
[21]As a black woman and single mother from the favelas, Marielle Franco positioned Marielle Franco as a representative and defender of poor black women and people from the favelas.
[22]With over 46,500 votes, Marielle Franco was one of the 51 representatives elected, receiving the fifth highest vote total out of more than 1,500 candidates.
[23]As a city council member, Marielle Franco fought against  violence against women, for reproductive rights, and for the rights of favela residents.
[24]Marielle Franco chaired the Women's Defense Commission and formed part of a four-person committee that monitored the federal intervention in Rio de Janeiro.
[25]Working with the Rio de Janeiro Lesbian Front, Marielle Franco presented a bill to create a day of lesbian visibility in Rio de Janeiro in August 2017, but the bill was defeated by a vote of 19–17.



[26]== Final days and assassination ==

On 13 March 2018, Marielle Franco spoke out on Twitter against the police violence in Rio de Janeiro: "Another homicide of a young man that could be credited to the police.
[27]Matheus Melo was leaving church when he was killed.
[28]How many others will have to die for this war to end?"The next day, Marielle Franco attended a round-table discussion titled "Young Black Women Moving [Power] Structures" (Portuguese: Jovens Negras Movendo Estruturas).
[29]Less than two hours after leaving the round-table, Marielle Franco and Marielle Franco's driver, Anderson Pedro Gomes, were fatally shot by two men who were driving another car.
[30]The men fired nine shots at them, four of which struck Marielle Franco – three in the head and one in the neck.
[31]Marielle Franco press officer was next to Marielle Franco's in the back seat and was injured, but survived.
[32]Marcelo Freixo, a Rio de Janeiro legislative assembly member from PSOL who came to the scene shortly after hearing of Marielle Franco's killing, determined that the bullets had been directed at Marielle Franco's in a clear execution.
[33]According to the Rio de Janeiro police, the direction of the nine shots supports the hypothesis that Marielle Franco was assassinated.
[34]The bullets that killed Marielle Franco are from a batch bought by the federal police in Brasília in 2006; Minister of Public Security Raul Jungmann later said that they were stolen from a post office storage facility in Paraíba, but the ministry subsequently retracted this explanation after the Post Office publicly denied it.
[35]In January 2019, police arrested Ronald Paulo Alves Pereira and issued a warrant for Adriano Magalhães da Nóbrega, both suspects in Marielle Franco assassination.
[36]Each suspect received honors from president Jair Bolsonaro's son Flávio Bolsonaro in the early 2000s.
[37]Nóbrega's wife and mother were still being employed in Flávio Bolsonaro's office in 2018. In March 2019, police arrested two suspects, former members of the military police allegedly tied to a vigilante militia, for the murder.
[38]Prior to their arrest, both suspects had pictures taken with former President Jair Bolsonaro, and one was his neighbor at a luxury apartment complex in Rio.
[39]In November 2019, following leaked reports that President Bolsonaro had been in touch with one of the main suspects of the shooting, a number of Brazilian media outlets reported that police were investigating possible ties of his second son, Carlos, to the case.
[40]In 2020, then governor of Rio de Janeiro Wilson Witzel admitted to have interfered in investigations of Marielle Franco's death on television.



[41]=== Reactions ===
Thousands took to the streets in coordinated protests across Brazil, and both Amnesty International and Human Rights Watch condemned Marielle Franco's killing.
[42]In the United States, Black feminist scholars published a statement honouring Marielle Franco as "yet another martyr" for the Movement for Black Lives.
[43]Journalist Glenn Greenwald, whose husband David Miranda was a fellow City Council member at the time of Marielle Franco's assassination and was a close personal friend of Marielle Franco  listed what he referred to as the "most important subjects to cover" regarding Marielle Franco assassination stating:

"Marielle Franco relentless and brave activism against the most lawless police battalions, Marielle Franco's opposition to military intervention, and, most threateningly of all, Marielle Franco's growing power as Marielle Franco "
The double murder of Marielle Franco and Marielle Franco's driver Anderson was the subject of condemnation across the political spectrum in Brazil.
[44]All presidential candidates in Brazil during the 2018 campaign condemned the crime, except for former President Jair Bolsonaro, who repeatedly declined to comment on the case; his campaign alleged that his views on the subject would be too controversial, despite his being the campaign that most focused on security issues prior to election day.
[45]In 2022, President Lula announced the appointment of Marielle Franco's sister, Anielle Franco, as Minister of Racial Equality.



[46]== Legacy ==
A digital art workshop was held in Nairobi, Kenya titled "Portraits of Marielle Franco  Creating Bridges between Kenya and Brazil" with young Kenyan artists participating, and the artworks were displayed at the Maré Museum in Rio de Janeiro on 10 November 2018.
[47]Amnesty International included Marielle Franco name in its campaign for those who wrote for human rights and lost their lives, while an update was made and an online tribute listed Marielle Franco among over 400 human rights advocates for women by the Association for Women's Rights in Development.
[48]A docu-series titled Marielle – O Documentário was released by Globoplay on 13 March 2020. Version 19 of antiX, a Linux distribution based on Debian Stable, is named after Marielle Franco
