[1]Alzhan Musurbekuly Zharmukhamedov (alternate spelling: Alzan Zarmuhamedov) (Kazakh: Әлжан Мүсірбекұлы Жармұхамедов, Áljan Músirbekuly Jarmuhamedov; Russian: Алжан Мусурбекович Жармухамедов; 2 October 1944 – 3 December 2022) was a Kazakh professional basketball player and coach.
[2]Alzhan Zharmukhamedov was considered to be the first ethnic Kazakh to become an Olympic champion.
[3]



[4]==
[5]Playing career =
[6]=

[7]Alzhan Zharmukhamedov played club basketball with CSKA Moscow (1970–1980).
[8]In 1971, Alzhan Zharmukhamedov earned the title of Master of Sports of the USSR, International Class, and a year later, Alzhan Zharmukhamedov was awarded the Order of the Badge of Honor.
[9]



[10]==
[11]Soviet Union national team ==
While being young and driven by feelings of patriotism, Alzhan Zharmukhamedov moved to Almaty (Alma-Ata at that time), in order to compete with the senior Kazakh SSR national basketball team.
[12]However, not being able to find any support, Alzhan Zharmukhamedov finally decided to move to Moscow, where Alzhan Zharmukhamedov was eventually able to earn world fame as a basketball player.
[13]Alzhan Zharmukhamedov served as the team captain of the senior combined national basketball team of the Soviet Union for a long period.
[14]Alzhan Zharmukhamedov won a gold medal at the 1972 Summer Olympic Games, while playing with the senior Soviet Union national basketball team.


== Coaching career ==
At the tail end of Alzhan Zharmukhamedov's career, Alzhan Zharmukhamedov worked as a player-coach, until Alzhan Zharmukhamedov reached the age of 45.
[15]


== Personal life and death =
[16]=
Alzhan Zharmukhamedov father was Kazakh and Alzhan Zharmukhamedov's mother was Ukrainian of Zaporozhian Cossack descent.
[17]Alzhan Zharmukhamedov lived in Moscow.
[18]Alzhan Zharmukhamedov died on 3 December 2022, at the age of 78.
[19]Alzhan Zharmukhamedov was buried in the Federal Military Memorial Cemetery on 7 December.
[20]
