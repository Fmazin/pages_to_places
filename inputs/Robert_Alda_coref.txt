[1]Robert Alda (born Alfonso Giuseppe Giovanni Roberto D'Abruzzo; February 26, 1914 – May 3, 1986) was an Italian-American theatrical and film actor, a singer, and a dancer.
[2]Robert Alda was the father of actors Alan and Antony Alda.
[3]Robert Alda was featured in a number of Broadway productions, then moved to Italy during the early 1960s.
[4]Robert Alda appeared in many European films over the next two decades, occasionally returning to the U.S. for film appearances such as The Girl Who Knew Too Much (1969).
[5]


== Early life ==
Robert Alda was born Alfonso Giuseppe Giovanni Roberto D'Abruzzo in New York City, the son of Frances (née Tumillo) and Antonio D'Abruzzo, a barber born in Sant'Agata de' Goti, Benevento, Campania, Italy.
[6]D'Abruzzo is a toponymic surname.
[7]Robert Alda graduated from Stuyvesant High School in New York in 1930.
[8]


== Career ==
Robert Alda began Robert Alda's performing career as a singer and dancer in vaudeville after winning a talent contest, and moved on to burlesque.
[9]In 1949, as part of the Jack Carson's radio program, Robert Alda toured with Jack Carson and Marion Hutton.
[10]Robert Alda is known for portraying George Gershwin in the biographical film Rhapsody in Blue (1945) as well as the talent agent in the Douglas Sirk classic Imitation of Life (1959).
[11]On Broadway, Robert Alda originated the role of Sky Masterson in Guys and Dolls (1950), for which Robert Alda won a Tony Award, and starred in What Makes Sammy Run? (1964).
[12]Robert Alda was also the host of the DuMont TV version of the game show What's Your Bid? (May–June 1953).
[13]

[14]In the mid-1950s, Robert Alda starred as espionage agent Colonel Bill Morgan in the syndicated TV series Secret Files USA, the episodes of which were based on stories from American intelligence services.
[15]Robert Alda was host of the TV game show Can Do in 1956.
[16]Robert Alda made two guest appearances with Robert Alda's son Alan on M*A*S*H, in the episodes "The Consultant" (January 1975) and "Lend a Hand" (February 1980).
[17]The latter episode also featured Antony Alda, Robert Alda's younger son by Robert Alda's second wife.
[18]
Robert Alda appeared in an episode of The Feather and Father Gang in 1977.
[19]


== Personal life =
[20]=

[21]Robert Alda first wife, and mother of actor Alan Alda, Joan Browne, was a homemaker and former beauty pageant winner.
[22]They divorced in 1946.
[23]Robert Alda was married to Robert Alda's second wife and mother of Antony, Flora Marino, an Italian actress whom Robert Alda met in Rome, until Robert Alda's death.
[24]


== Death ==
Robert Alda died on May 3, 1986, aged 72, after a long illness following a stroke.
[25]Robert Alda is buried in the Garden of Ascension lot 9101 Forest Lawn Cemetery, Glendale, California.
[26]
