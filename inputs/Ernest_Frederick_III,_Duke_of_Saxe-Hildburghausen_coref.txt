[1]Ernest Frederick III Karl, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen (10 June 1727 in Königsberg in Bayern – 23 September 1780 in Seidingstadt), was a duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen.
[2]


== Biography ==
Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen was born on 10 June 1727.
[3]Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen was the eldest son of Ernst Frederick II, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen and Caroline of Erbach-Fürstenau.
[4]

[5]Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen succeeded Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen's father as Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen when Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen was only eighteen years old in 1745; as a result Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen's mother, the Dowager Duchess Caroline, acted as a regent on Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen's behalf until Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen reached adulthood, in 1748.
Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen was considered to be intelligent, talented, and one of the most handsome princes of Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen's time.
[6]Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen donated a library to the city, but finally Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen's excessive prodigality in exaggerated court pomp and military splendor drew the attention of the highest places to the financial situation of Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen's country.
[7]

[8]The Emperor Joseph II created a debit commission under management of the Duchess Charlotte Amalie of Saxe-Meiningen and prince Joseph of Saxe-Hildburghausen, the granduncle of Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen  to investigate the demands of the creditors and adjust the incomes and expenditures to 1769.
[9]The financial situation of the country was so disastrous that 35 years duration of this commission could not repair conditions completely.
[10]
After Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen made use in 1757 of the Münzregal (Imperial coinage regale), Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen was entangled in a complaint of the realm treasury.
[11]Finally, the huge fire of the city of Hildburghausen in 1779, forced Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen to move to Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen's hunting residence in Seidingstadt, where Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen died a year later.
[12]


== Family ==
In the Hirschholm Palace, north of Copenhagen on 1 October 1749, Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen was first married to Princess Louise of Denmark, daughter of the King Christian VI.
[13]They had one daughter:

Princess Friederike Sophie Juliane Karoline of Saxe-Hildburghausen (b. Hildburghausen, 5 December 1755 – d. Hildburghausen, 10 January 1756).
[14]In the Christiansborg Palace, Copenhagen on 20 January 1757, five months after the death of Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen's first wife, Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen was married for the second time to Christiane Sophie Charlotte of Brandenburg-Bayreuth.
[15]They had one daughter:

Princess Friederike Sophie Marie Karoline of Saxe-Hildburghausen (b. Seidingstadt, 4 October 1757 – d. Seidingstadt, 17 October 1757).
[16]In Bayreuth on 1 July 1758, nine months after the death of Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen's second wife, Ernest Frederick III, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen was married for the third time to Ernestine, a daughter of Duke Ernst August I of Saxe-Weimar.
[17]They had three children:

Princess Ernestine Frederike Sophie of Saxe-Hildburghausen (b. Hildburghausen, 22 February 1760 – d. Coburg, 28 October 1776), married on 6 March 1776 to Franz Frederick Anton, Duke of Saxe-Coburg-Saalfeld.
[18]She died childless only six months after her wedding.
[19]

[20]Princess Christiane Sophie Caroline of Saxe-Hildburghausen (b. Hildburghausen, 4 December 1761 – d. Öhringen, 10 January 1790), married on 13 March 1778 to her uncle Eugen of Saxe-Hildeburghausen, who was her father's own brother.
[21]They had no children.
[22]

[23]Frederick, Duke of Saxe-Hildburghausen (b. Hildburghausen, 29 April 1763 – d. Jagdhaus Hummelshain, Altenburg, 29 September 1834).
[24]He married Charlotte Georgine of Mecklenburg-Strelitz, sister of the queens of Prussia and Hanover; they became the parents of 12 children
