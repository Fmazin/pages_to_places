[1]Friedrich Wilhelm II (12 February 1603, in Weimar – 22 April 1669, in Altenburg), was a duke of Saxe-Altenburg.
[2]

[3]Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg was the youngest son of Friedrich Wilhelm I, Duke of Saxe-Weimar, and Anna Maria of the Palatinate-Neuburg, his second wife.
[4]Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg was born eight months after the death of Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg's father, on 7 July 1602.
[5]
Shortly after Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg's birth, Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg and Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg's older brothers inherited Saxe-Altenburg as co-rulers under the guardianship of the Electors of Saxony Christian II and John George I until 1618, when Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg's older brother John Philip assumed the government of the duchy and the guardianship of his younger siblings.
[6]


[7]By 1632, two of Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg's three brothers were deceased.
[8]Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg and Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg's older brother John Philip began a joint government; but, in fact, John Philip was the real and only ruler of the duchy of Saxe-Altenburg.
[9]Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg was only a nominal ruler until the death of John Philip (1639), when Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg began Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg's personal reign over Altenburg.
[10]

[11]At Altenburg Castle on 18 September 1638 Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg married Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg's first wife, Sophie Elisabeth, the only daughter of Christian William of Brandenburg.
[12]She died in 1650 after twelve years of childless marriage.
[13]

[14]In Dresden on 11 October 1652 Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg married secondly Magdalene Sibylle of Saxony, Dowager Crown Princess of Denmark and daughter of Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg's former regent, the Elector John George I.
[15]They had three children:

Christian (b. Altenburg, 27 February 1654 – d. Altenburg, 5 June 1663).
[16]Painting of Christian
Johanna Magdalena (b. Altenburg, 14 January 1656 – d. Weissenfels, 22 January 1686), married on 25 October 1671 to Johann Adolf I, Duke of Saxe-Weissenfels.
[17]
Friedrich Wilhelm III, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg (b. Altenburg, 12 July 1657 – d. Altenburg, 14 April 1672).In 1660 he acquired the towns of Themar and Meiningen.
[18]In 1664 Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg built a hunting seat (Jagdschloss) in Hummelshain, and, in 1665, Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg built a particularly beautiful widow seat (Witwensitz) called "Magdalenenstift" in Altenburg for Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg's wife Magdalene Sybille.
[19]
After Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg's death Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg was succeeded by Friedrich Wilhelm II, Duke of Saxe-Altenburg's second and only surviving son, Frederick William III.
