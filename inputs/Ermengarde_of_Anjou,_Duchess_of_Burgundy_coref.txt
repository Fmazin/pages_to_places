[1]Ermengarde of Anjou (c. 1018 – 18 March 1076), known as Blanche, was a Duchess consort of Burgundy.
[2]Ermengarde of Anjou, Duchess of Burgundy was the daughter of Count Fulk III of Anjou and Hildegarde of Sundgau.
[3]Ermengarde of Anjou, Duchess of Burgundy was sometimes known as Ermengarde-Blanche.
[4]Ermengarde of Anjou, Duchess of Burgundy married Geoffrey II, Count of Gâtinais c. 1035 (in French Geoffroy), called Ferréol and sometimes known as Aubri, seigneur de Château-Landon.
[5]Together they had:

Hildegarde de Château-Landon, married c.1060 to Joscelin I, Lord of Courtenay
Geoffrey III, Count of Anjou
Fulk IV, Count of AnjouGeoffrey II died sometime between 1043 and 1046, and Ermengarde of Anjou, Duchess of Burgundy mother died while on pilgrimage to Jerusalem in 1046.
[6]

[7]Ermengarde of Anjou, Duchess of Burgundy married Robert I, Duke of Burgundy in 1046, conferring on Ermengarde of Anjou, Duchess of Burgundy's the title Duchess of Burgundy.
[8] 
[9]They had:

Hildegarde (c. 1056–1104), who married Duke William VIII of Aquitaine around 1067 Ermengarde of Anjou, Duchess of Burgundy died 18 March 1076, at the Church of Fleurey-sur-Ouche.
