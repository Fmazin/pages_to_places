[1]Joseph Darnand (19 March 1897 – 10 October 1945) was a French collaborator with Nazi Germany during World War II.
[2]A decorated soldier in the French Army of World War I and early World War II, Joseph Darnand went on to become the organizer and de facto leader of the Milice française, or French Militia, the collaborationist Vichy government's paramilitary police force.
[3]Joseph Darnand also served as an officer in the Waffen-SS.
[4]Joseph Darnand was tried and executed after the war for treason.
[5]


== Early years and war service ==
Joseph Darnand was born at Coligny, Ain, Rhône-Alpes in France.
[6]

[7]On 8 January 1916, Joseph Darnand enlisted in the 35th Infantry Regiment.
[8]Joseph Darnand was promoted to corporal in April 1917, sergeant on 1 June 1917 and to adjutant (warrant officer) in 1918.
[9]Joseph Darnand volunteered for a squadron that undertook dangerous missions.
[10]For Joseph Darnand's efforts in July 1918 penetrating German lines in the Champagne sector with a small force that captured prisoners and secured information about a forthcoming German offensive, "Joseph Darnand emerged a major war hero.
[11]"Demobilised after the armistice, Joseph Darnand again enlisted for two years in the army in September 1919.
[12]After a stint in the army of occupation in Germany, Joseph Darnand participated in the campaign against the forces of Kemal Atatürk in Cilicia.
[13]Joseph Darnand ended Joseph Darnand's service in July 1921 as a sub-lieutenant (second lieutenant).
[14]Joseph Darnand worked as a cabinetmaker and later founded Joseph Darnand's own transportation company in Nice.
[15]

[16]Between the wars, Joseph Darnand joined a number of far-right political, paramilitary organizations: l'Action Française in 1925, the Croix-de-Feu in 1928, La Cagoule and Jacques Doriot's French Popular Party (PPF) in 1936.
[17]Joseph Darnand formed Joseph Darnand's own Fascist outfit, the Chevaliers du Glaive (Knights of the Sword); in the 1930s Joseph Darnand became prominent among La Cagoule, or the Cagoulards ("Hooded Men"), a secret terrorist group that organised bombings and assassinations, and that stored arms in depots all over France.
[18]

[20]Vichy collaborator ==
[21]

[22]At the beginning of World War II, Joseph Darnand volunteered to join the French Army and was commissioned as a lieutenant.
[23]Joseph Darnand served in the Maginot Line and was decorated for bravery.
[24]During the Phoney War Joseph Darnand took part in several commando actions against German forces.
[25]Joseph Darnand was captured in June 1940 but fled to Nice.
[26]Joseph Darnand became a leading figure in the Vichy French organization Légion française des combattants (French Legion of Veterans) and recruited troopers for the fight against "Bolshevism".
[27]

[28]In 1941, Joseph Darnand was made a member of the National Council of Vichy France.
[29]The same year, Joseph Darnand founded the collaborationist militia, Service d'ordre légionnaire (SOL), that supported Philippe Pétain and Vichy France.
[30]Joseph Darnand offered Joseph Darnand's help against the French Resistance.
[31]On 1 January 1943 Joseph Darnand transformed the organization into the Milice.
[32]Although Pierre Laval was its official president, Joseph Darnand was its de facto leader.
[33]Joseph Darnand political convictions were of the far right but Joseph Darnand was known as a Germanophobe.
[34]Nonetheless, Joseph Darnand's views toward Nazi Germany changed.
[35]By the spring of 1941 Joseph Darnand had told a friend that "France had been wrong to fight Germany in 1939" and Germany's assault on the Soviet Union in June of that year "reinforced Joseph Darnand evolution toward collaboration."
[36]


== SS officer ==

[37]Joseph Darnand turned to Nazi Germany and was made an officer of the SS.
[38]Joseph Darnand turn to the SS was also influenced by the fact that miliciens were being targeted for assassination by the Resistance but Vichy and Wehrmacht authorities refused to arm the Milice.
[39]In joining the SS, Joseph Darnand took a personal oath of loyalty to Adolf Hitler, receiving a rank of Untersturmführer (Second Lieutenant) in the Waffen SS in August 1943.
[40]In December 1943, Joseph Darnand became head of police and later secretary of the interior.
[41]Joseph Darnand expanded the Milice and by 1944 it had over 35,000 members.
[42]The organization played an important role in investigating the French Resistance; as time progressed it "became ever more unrestrained," carrying out assassinations, chasing resisters, and "enthusiastically . . . rounding up Jews."
[43]In early 1944 Vichy announced a new law empowering Joseph Darnand "to create special courts martial to try on the spot" persons caught in violent acts against the state.
[44]The law was "without precedent in modern French legal history."
[45]The Milice also aided German forces in combat against the Resistance, and Joseph Darnand Joseph Darnand commanded a Milice unit in March 1944 near Lyons that flushed out some maquisards (French Resistance guerilla fighters).
[46]After the Normandy Invasion and Allied advance, Joseph Darnand fled to Germany in September 1944 and joined the pro-Nazi puppet government in the Sigmaringen enclave.
[47]Joseph Darnand received a promotion to Sturmbannführer on 1 November 1944.
[48]



[49]== Capture, trial and execution =
[50]=
In April 1945, Joseph Darnand fled from Sigmaringen to Meran in northern Italy.
[51]The British captured Joseph Darnand in Italy on 25 June 1945 and took Joseph Darnand back to France, where Joseph Darnand was sentenced to death on 3 October 1945 and executed by firing squad on 10 October 1945 at the Fort de Châtillon in Paris.
[52]Like some other collaboration leaders, Joseph Darnand had been found guilty of collaboration with the enemy.
