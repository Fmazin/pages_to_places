[1]Alexander Karađorđević (Serbian Cyrillic: Александар Карађорђевић, romanized: Aleksandar Karađorđević; 11 October 1806 – 3 May 1885) was the prince of Serbia between 1842 and 1858 and a member of the House of Karađorđević.
[2]


== Early life ==

[3]Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia was born in Topola on 11 October 1806.
[4]Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia was educated in Khotin, Bessarabia (Russia), under the patronage of the Russian Tsar.
[5]

[6]After the Sultan’s decree acknowledging the title of Prince Mihailo Obrenović at the end of 1839, the family returned to Serbia.
[7]Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia joined the Headquarters of the Serbian Army, and was promoted to the rank of Lieutenant and appointed as adjutant to Prince Mihailo.
[8]


== Prince of Serbia ==
After the political conflicts caused by disrespect of the so-called "Turkish constitution," and Miloš Obrenović's and then Mihailo Obrenović's abdications, Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia was elected the Prince of Serbia at the National Assembly in Vračar, a municipality in modern Belgrade, on 14 September 1842.
[9]Having had Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia's title acknowledged by Russia and Turkey, Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia started the reforms and founded a number of new institutions in order to improve the progress of the Serbian state.
[10]Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia implemented the code of civil rights, introduced the regular Army, built a cannon foundry, improved the existing schools and founded new ones, as well as established the National Library and National Museum.
[11]

[12]It was Councillor Lazar Arsenijević Batalaka who, in 1845 introduced Ilija Garašanin to Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia 
[13]During the Hungarian Revolution in Vojvodina, in 1848, Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia sent Serbian volunteers under the command of Stevan Knićanin to help the Serbs’ struggle for autonomy.
[14]As a follow-up of the national-political movements of 1848, the pan-slavistic idea of a Yugoslav Monarchy emerged.
[15]The "Načertanije" (the "Draft") document, written as a Serbian political program by Ilija Garašanin four years earlier, made the mission of replacing the Austrian and Turkish domination of all Southern Slavs with the Serbian rule under the banner of "Serbia."
[16]

[17]Throughout Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia's reign, Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia was troubled with Obrenović plots.
[18]By Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia's refusal to take part in the Crimean War as an ally of the French, British and Ottoman Empires against the Russian Empire.
[19]The result was Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia's overthrow and departure into exile in 1858 by the winners of the Powers in the war and bringing the rival Obrenović dynasty to the throne of the Principality of Serbia.
[20]
Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia was awarded the Ottoman Order of Glory and Order of Distinction.

The popularity of the pro-Austrian Prince Alexander Karađorđević,  declined dramatically after the Treaty of Paris left Serbia with no concessions at the end of the Crimean War.
[28]The Assembly voted upon Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia's deposition on December 23, 1858, and Miloš Obrenović, who had previously been Prince of Serbia was recalled.
[29]In contrast to the pro-Austrian policies of Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia  Obrenović stood firm against both Turkey and Austria.
[30]He managed to pressure the Assembly to grant the House of Obrenović succession to the Serbian throne.
[31]Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia died in Timișoara on 3 May 1885.
[32]Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia was buried in Vienna, and Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia's earthly remains were moved in 1912 to the Memorial Church of St. George built by Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia's son Petar I Karađorđević, in Oplenac, Serbia.

[34]== Marriage and issue ==
On 1 June 1830 in Hotin, Bessarabia, Alexander Karađorđević, Prince of Serbia married Persida Nenadović (15 February 1813 – 29 March 1873), member of the powerful Nenadović family, daughter of Voivode Jevrem Nenadović (1793–1867) and Jovanka Milovanović (1792–1880).
[35]They had ten children:

Princess Poleksija (1 February 1833 – 5 December 1914), married firstly in 1849 Konstantin Nikolajević (1821 – suicide 13 October 1877), Minister of the Interior of Serbia, by whom she had issue; secondly Dr Alexander Preshern (1830 – 2 December 1914).
[36]

[37]Princess Kleopatra (26 November 1835 – 13 July 1855), married in 1855 Milan Avram Petronijević, Serbian Ambassador to Russia.
Prince Aleksij (23 March 1836 – 21 April 1841)
Prince Svetozar (1841 – 17 March 1847)
Prince Peter (29 June 1844 – 16 August 1921), ruled Serbia from 1903 until 1918, and subsequently as King of the Kingdom of Serbs, Croats, and Slovenes until his death; married Princess Zorka of Montenegro, by whom he had issue.
[38]

[39]Princess Jelena (18 October 1846 – 26 July 1867), married in 1867 Đorđe Simić (28 February 1843 – 11 October 1921), Prime Minister of Serbia.
[40]

[41]Prince Andrej (15 September 1848 – 12 July 1864)
Princess Jelisaveta (born and died 1850)
Prince Đorđe (11 October 1856 – 5 January 1889)
Prince Arsenije (16 April 1859 – 1938), married in 1892, Princess Aurora of San Donato.
[42]They were the parents of Prince Paul, Regent of Yugoslavia.
