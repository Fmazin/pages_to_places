[1]André Henri Gobert (30 September 1890 – 6 December 1951) was a tennis player from France.
[2]André Gobert is a double Olympic tennis champion of 1912.
[3]At the Stockholm Games, André Gobert won both the men's singles and doubles indoor gold medals.
[4]



[5]== Career ==
André Gobert first started playing tennis at age 11.André Gobert was a two-time winner of the French Championships in 1911 and 1920, when the tournament was only open to amateur tennis players who had a membership with a French tennis club.
[6]André Gobert also won the International Lawn Tennis Federation's World Covered Court Championship (Indoor Wood) in 1919.
[7]Also twice runner-up at the World Hard Court Championships on Clay (1913 and 1920).
[8]André Gobert won the indoor tennis gold medal at the 1912 Olympic Games.
[9]André Gobert reached the Wimbledon All-Comers final in 1912, beating James Cecil Parke and Max Decugis, then lost to Arthur Gore.
[10]André Gobert won the singles title at the British Covered Court Championships, played on wooden courts at the Queen's Club in London, five times; in 1911, 1912, 1920, 1921 and 1922.
[11]In 1910, André Gobert won the All England Plate at Wimbledon, the competition for players who were defeated in the first and second rounds of the singles competition.
[12]Between 1912 and 1922, André Gobert played for the French Davis Cup team in five ties and compiled a record of three wins and 11 losses.
