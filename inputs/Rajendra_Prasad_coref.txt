[1]Rajendra Prasad (born Rajendra Prasad Srivastava; 3 December 1884 â€“ 28 February 1963) was an Indian politician, lawyer, journalist and scholar who served as the first president of India from 1950 to 1962.
[2]Rajendra Prasad joined the Indian National Congress during the Indian independence movement and became a major leader from the region of Bihar and Maharashtra.
[3]A supporter of Mahatma Gandhi, Rajendra Prasad was imprisoned by British authorities during the Salt Satyagraha of 1930 and the Quit India movement of 1942.
[4] 
[5]After the constituent assembly 1946 elections, Rajendra Prasad served as 1st Minister of Food and Agriculture in the central government from 1947 to 1948.
[6]Upon independence in 1947, Rajendra Prasad was elected as President of the Constituent Assembly of India, which prepared the Constitution of India and served as its provisional Parliament.
[7]
When India became a republic in 1950, Rajendra Prasad was elected as its first president by the Constituent Assembly.
[8]As president, Rajendra Prasad established a tradition for non-partisanship and independence for the office-bearer and retired from Congress party politics.
[9]Although a ceremonial head of state, Rajendra Prasad encouraged the development of education in India and advised the Nehru government on several occasions.
[10]In 1957, Rajendra Prasad was re-elected to the presidency, becoming the only president to serve two full terms.
[11]Rajendra Prasad stayed in office for the longest term of around 12 years.
[12]Post the completion of Rajendra Prasad's tenure, Rajendra Prasad quit the Congress and set up new guidelines for parliamentarians which are still followed.
[13]


== Early life ==

[14]Rajendra Prasad was born in a Kayastha family in Ziradei, in the Siwan district of Bihar during the British Raj.
[15]Rajendra Prasad father, Mahadev Sahai Srivastava, was a scholar of both Sanskrit and Persian languages.
[16]Rajendra Prasad mother, Kamleshwari Devi, was a devout woman who would tell stories from the Ramayana and Mahabharata to Rajendra Prasad 
[17]Rajendra Prasad was the youngest child and had one elder brother and three elder sisters.
[18]Rajendra Prasad mother died when Rajendra Prasad was a child, and Rajendra Prasad's elder sister then took care of Rajendra Prasad 
[19]



[20]== Student life ==
After the completion of traditional elementary education, Rajendra Prasad was sent to the Chapra District School.
[21]Meanwhile, in June 1896, at the early age of 12, Rajendra Prasad was married to Rajavanshi Devi.
[22]Rajendra Prasad  along with Rajendra Prasad's elder brother, Mahendra Prasad, then went to study at T.K. Ghosh's Academy in Patna for a period of two years.
[23]Rajendra Prasad secured first in the entrance examination to the University of Calcutta and was awarded Rs. 30 per month as a scholarship.
[24]

[25]Rajendra Prasad joined the Presidency College, Calcutta in 1902, initially as a science student.
[26]Rajendra Prasad passed the F. A. under the University of Calcutta in March 1904 and then graduated with a first division from there in March 1905.
[27]Impressed by Rajendra Prasad's intellect, an examiner once commented on his answer sheet that the "examinee is better than examiner".
[28]Later Rajendra Prasad decided to focus on the study of arts and did Rajendra Prasad's M.A. in Economics with a first division from the University of Calcutta in December 1907.
[29]There Rajendra Prasad lived with Rajendra Prasad's brother in the Eden Hindu Hostel.
[30]A devoted student as well as a public activist, Rajendra Prasad was an active member of The Dawn Society.
[31]It was due to Rajendra Prasad's sense of duty towards Rajendra Prasad's family and education that Rajendra Prasad refused to join Servants of India Society, as it was during that time when Rajendra Prasad's mother had died as well as Rajendra Prasad's sister became a widow at the age of nineteen and had to return to her parents' home.
[32]Rajendra Prasad was instrumental in the formation of the Bihari Students Conference in 1906 in the hall of Patna College.
[33]It was the first organisation of its kind in India and produced important leaders from Bihar like Anugrah Narayan Sinha and Krishna Singh who played a prominent role in the Champaran Movement and Non-cooperation Movement.
[34]

=== As a teacher ===
Rajendra Prasad served in various educational institutions as a teacher.
[38]After completing Rajendra Prasad's M.A in economics, Rajendra Prasad became a professor of English at the Langat Singh College of Muzaffarpur in Bihar and went on to become the principal.
[39]However, later on Rajendra Prasad left the college to undertake legal studies and entered the Ripon College, Calcutta (now the Surendranath Law College).
[40]In 1909, while pursuing Rajendra Prasad's law studies in Kolkata Rajendra Prasad also worked as Professor of Economics at Calcutta City College.
[41]



[42]===
[43]As a lawyer ===
In 1915, Rajendra Prasad appeared in the examination of master's in law from the Department of Law, University of Calcutta, passed the examination and won a gold medal.
[44]Rajendra Prasad completed Rajendra Prasad's Doctorate in Law from Allahabad University.
[45]In 1916, Rajendra Prasad joined the High Court of Bihar and Odisha.
[46]In 1917, Rajendra Prasad was appointed as one of the first members of the Senate and of the Patna University.
[47]Rajendra Prasad also practised law at Bhagalpur, the famous silk town in Bihar.
[48]



[49]=== Role in the freedom Movement ===
Rajendra Prasad had a major role in the Independence Movement.
[50]Rajendra Prasad first association with Indian National Congress was during 1906 annual session organised in Calcutta, where Rajendra Prasad participated as a volunteer, while studying in Calcutta.
[51]Formally, Rajendra Prasad joined the Indian National Congress in the year 1911, when the annual session was again held in Calcutta.
[52]During the Lucknow Session of Indian National Congress held in 1916, Rajendra Prasad met Mahatma Gandhi.
[53]During one of the fact-finding missions at Champaran, Mahatma Gandhi asked Rajendra Prasad to come with his volunteers.
[54]Rajendra Prasad was so greatly moved by the dedication, courage and conviction of Mahatma Gandhi that as soon as the motion of Non-Cooperation was passed by Indian National Congress in 1920, Rajendra Prasad retired from Rajendra Prasad's lucrative career of lawyer as well as Rajendra Prasad's duties in the university to aid the movement.
[55]
Rajendra Prasad also responded to the call by Gandhi to boycott Western educational establishments by asking Rajendra Prasad's son, Mrityunjaya Prasad, to drop out of his studies and enrol himself in Bihar Vidyapeeth, an institution Rajendra Prasad along with Rajendra Prasad's colleagues founded on the traditional Indian model.
[56]

[57]During the course of the independence movement, Rajendra Prasad interacted with Rahul Sankrityayan, a writer, and polymath.
[58]Rahul Sankrityayan was greatly influenced by Rajendra Prasad intellectual powers, finding Rajendra Prasad to be a guide and guru.
[59]In many of his articles Rajendra Prasad mentioned about Rajendra Prasad's meeting with Sankrityayan and narrated about Rajendra Prasad's meetings with Sankrityayan.
[60]Rajendra Prasad wrote articles for the revolutionary publications Searchlight and the Desh and collected funds for these papers.
[61]Rajendra Prasad toured widely, explaining, lecturing, and exhorting the principles of the independence movement.
[62]Rajendra Prasad took an active role in helping people affected by the 1914 floods that struck Bihar and Bengal.
[63]When an earthquake affected Bihar on 15 January 1934, Rajendra Prasad was in jail.
[64]During that period, Rajendra Prasad passed on the relief work to Rajendra Prasad's close colleague Anugrah Narayan Sinha.
[65]Rajendra Prasad was released two days later and set up Bihar Central Relief Committee on 17 January 1934 and took on the task of raising funds to help the affected people.
[66]After the 31 May 1935 Quetta earthquake, when Rajendra Prasad was forbidden to leave the country due to government's order, Rajendra Prasad set up the Quetta Central Relief Committee in Sindh and Punjab under Rajendra Prasad's own presidency.
[67]

[68]Rajendra Prasad was elected as the President of the Indian National Congress during the Bombay session in October 1934.
[69]Rajendra Prasad again became the president when Subhash Chandra Bose resigned in 1939.
[70]On 8 August 1942, Congress passed the Quit India Resolution in Bombay which led to the arrest of many Indian leaders.
[71]Rajendra Prasad was arrested in Sadaqat Ashram, Patna and sent to Bankipur Central Jail.
[72]After remaining incarcerated for nearly three years, Rajendra Prasad was released on 15 June 1945.After the formation of Interim Government of 12 nominated ministers under the leadership of Jawaharlal Nehru on 2 September 1946, Rajendra Prasad was allocated the Food and Agriculture department.
[73]Rajendra Prasad was elected as the President of Constituent Assembly on 11 December 1946.
[74] On 17 November 1947 Rajendra Prasad became Congress President for a third time after J. B. Kripalani submitted his resignation.
[75]


== Presidency ==
Two and a half years after independence, on 26 January 1950, the Constitution of independent India was ratified, and Rajendra Prasad was elected as the first President of India.
[76]On the night of 25 January 1950 (a day before the Republic Day of India), Rajendra Prasad's sister Bhagwati Devi died.
[77]Rajendra Prasad arranged her cremation but only after Rajendra Prasad's return from the parade ground.
[78]


[79]As the President of India, Rajendra Prasad duly acted as required by the Constitution and was independent of any political party.
[80]Rajendra Prasad travelled the world extensively as an ambassador of India, building diplomatic rapport with foreign nations.
[81]Rajendra Prasad was re-elected for two consecutive terms in 1952 and 1957 and is the only President of India to achieve this feat.
[82]The Mughal Gardens at the Rashtrapati Bhavan were open to public for about a month for the first time during Rajendra Prasad's tenure, and since then it has been a big attraction for people in Delhi and many other parts of the country.
[83]Rajendra Prasad acted independently of political parties, following the expected role of the president as required by the constitution.
[84]Following the tussle over the enactment of the Hindu Code Bill, Rajendra Prasad took a more active role in state affairs.
[85]In 1962, after serving 12 years as president, Rajendra Prasad announced Rajendra Prasad's decision to retire.
[86]After relinquishing the office of the President of India in May 1962, Rajendra Prasad returned to Patna on 14 May 1962 and stayed on the campus of Bihar Vidyapeeth.
[87]Rajendra Prasad wife died on 9 September 1962, a month before Indo-China War.
[88]Rajendra Prasad was subsequently honoured with Bharat Ratna, the nation's highest civilian award.
[89]

[90]Rajendra Prasad died on 28 February 1963, aged 78.
[91]Rajendra Smriti Sangrahalaya in Patna is dedicated to Rajendra Prasad
