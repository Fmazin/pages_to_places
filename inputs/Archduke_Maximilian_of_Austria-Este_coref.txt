[1]Archduke Maximilian Joseph of Austria-Este  (July 14, 1782 – June 1, 1863), the fourth son of Archduke Ferdinand of Austria-Este and younger brother of Francis IV, Duke of Modena.
[2].
[3] Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este was grand master of the Teutonic Knights from 1835 to 1863.
[4]


== Biography ==
Born in Milan, Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este was the son of Archduke Ferdinand of Austria (son of Maria Theresa of Austria and governor of the Duchy of Milan) and Maria Beatrice Ricciarda d'Este.
[5]Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este spent Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este's youth in Monza, where Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este's family had fled after the French invasion of the Duchy of Modena.
[6]After staying in Verona, Padua, Trieste and Laibach, Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este's family moved to Wiener Neustadt.
[7]

[8]In 1801 Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este joined the Teutonic Order, obtaining the Austrian Cross in 1804.
[9]After studying in the Theresian Military Academy of Wiener Neustadt, Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este was named Major General in the Austrian Army (1805).
[10]In 1809 Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este fought in Germany against the French; Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este clashed with the Napoleonic troops at Regensburg, leading Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este's army towards Linz.
[11]In 1819 Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este was elected a Royal Fellow of the Royal Society.
[12]In 1830 Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este established Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este in the Ebenzweier Castle, near Altmünster am Traunsee, while from 1831 to 1839 Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este lived in Linz.
[13]In 1835 Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este was named Grand Master of the Teutonic Order.
[14]
Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este erected several fortifications in the Austrian possessions in Italy, such as the Torri Massimiliane of Verona and the Torre Massimiliana of Venice.
[15]

[16]Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este died in 1863 in the Ebenzweier castle.
[17]Archduke Maximilian of Austria-Este is buried in Altmünster.
